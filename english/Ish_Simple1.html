Simple form of Verb - 1
 
The Simple form of a verb has a 's' (or 'es' rarely) added at the end of the present tense of the verb.
The 's' is added only when the verb refers to a 3rd person, singular.
's' is NOT added to the present tense of the verb when the verb is referring to I, you and plurals.

Example
Verb
 3rd Person ,Singular
Verb+s		 I , You and Plural
Eat He eats.
She eats.
It eats.
Shiva eats.
Bird eats. 		I eat.
You eat.
We eat.
They eat.
Shiva and Guha eat.
Birds eat.
Simple form is generally used when the tense is not critical or clear.
Eg: Bird eats.  Eating could be in the past, at present or in the future.

Some verbs with unusual simple forms.
go-> goes ,    do-> does,    have-> has
