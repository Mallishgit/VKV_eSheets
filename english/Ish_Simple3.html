Simple form - 3
 

Generally two simple forms cannot occur in a sentence without a conjunction between them.
If there is no conjunction then only the first verb is of the simple form.
In the presence of a conjunction (and,or, but) both verbs are in simple form.

Example
Verb
 Statement
Stand
Reach
 I stand on the ladder and reach the bird.
She stands on the ladder and reaches the bird.
They stand on the ladder and reach the bird.
Simple form is generally used when the tense is not critical or clear.
Eg: Bird eats.  Eating could be in the past, at present or in the future.
