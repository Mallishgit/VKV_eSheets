Common Factors - 5
 
Find a factor that can be factored out from all the given terms. 
Maintain the sequence of appearance of terms.
When multiple answers are correct , you can write any one of them.


Example:
1) 3a, 4a, 17a =>    a (3, 4, 17) 
2) b6 , b2,  b5   =>     b2  ( b4 , 1 ,b3 )    or      b  ( b5  , b , b4 )   . Both answers are correct. You could write either of them.
3) ab, ac, ad     a ( b , c , d )   . Although    a  ( c,  d, b )    too is a correct answer , it will be considered wrong for NOT maintaining the sequence.
