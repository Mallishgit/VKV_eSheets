Common Factors - 4
 
  "Factoring out the common" is a way of rewriting a given expression by taking out the common factor.
Eg: If 6 has to be factored out from these numbers 36, 18, 12  then it is written as follows...
36 18 12
6(6) 6(3) 6(2) Note What is written outside the bracket is 6, the term to be factored out.
 
Similarly, if m2 is to be factored out of the terms m3 4m3 2m4.
m3 4m3 2m4
m2(m) m2(4m) m2(2m2) Note What is written outside the bracket is m2, the term to be factored out.
 
Rewrite the given terms when the mentioned term is factored out. 
Example:
1)  Factor out 12 from 84 =>    12 (7) 
2)  Factor out  b2 from  b6  =>     b2  ( b4 )
