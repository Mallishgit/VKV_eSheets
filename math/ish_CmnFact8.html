Common Factors - 8
 
When a factor is multiplied with the terms from which they are factored we get the original terms/
The original terms are nb+nc+nd  .
When n is factored out it can be written as    n ( b + c + d )   .
Now if you multiply n with the terms (b , c, d)  =>  n (  b+c+d)
                                                                                    
We get back the original terms nb+nc+nd

Verify if multiplying the factor with the terms gives back the original terms.
Maintain the sequence of appearance of the terms.
 Example: 
 1) Original Terms: . Its factored form is  2n(3n+1+4b) .
 Here    2n   is factored out. When multiplied, the terms are   6n2+2n+8nb , which
   is  same as the Original Terms. So 2n,     is  a true factor.

 2) Original Terms: (6n2+2n-9nb) . Its factored form is  3n(2n,1,4nb) .
 Here    3n   is factored out. When multiplied, the terms are   6n2+3n-12nb  , which
   is NOT   same as the Original Terms. So,   3n    is NOT   a true factor.
