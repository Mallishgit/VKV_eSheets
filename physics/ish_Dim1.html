System International.
  When involved in tasks where measurements are important we need to agree upon using same units. This is needed for the ease of understanding and to avoid mistakes. Look at this conversation between Shiva and Tarun.

Shivaraj(S)

T: I worked in the field for 180 minutes
S: I worked in the field for just 3 hours

Who has worked more?
Both have worked for the same duration (amount of time). However, since the units are different it may not be clear at first look.

T: My coffee is 40 o c
S: My coffee is 303 o k

Whose coffee is hotter?
Tarun's coffee is hotter than Shivaraj's. However, since the units are different it may not be clear at first look.

Hence the need for using common units. System International is the world's most widely used system of measurement. Measurements in non-standard units are converted to SI units before calculating.
